# Chapter 5 Tokenomics Research Map

This note supports Chapter 5, "Tokenomics Analysis," by mapping the chapter's claims to evidence requirements, research questions, risk categories, and future validation criteria. It is intentionally cautious because tokenomics analysis requires confirmed supply data, governance records, incentive mechanics, and post-deployment network activity.

## Purpose

Chapter 5 should evaluate QoreChain tokenomics as an incentive system rather than as a market narrative. The chapter should avoid price speculation, reward certainty, or promotional statements. Its academic role is to examine how token utility, validator incentives, delegation, light node participation, governance, and ecosystem rewards may affect network sustainability.

## Research Questions

| Research Question | Why It Matters | Evidence Needed |
|---|---|---|
| What confirmed utilities does QOR have within the network? | Token utility affects demand, participation, and network function | Official protocol documentation, fee model, staking/governance documentation |
| How are validators, delegators, and light node operators incentivized? | Infrastructure incentives affect reliability and decentralization | Reward mechanics, commission model, uptime requirements, validator documentation |
| How are quest points separated from token rewards or commissions? | Community campaigns should not be confused with guaranteed economic rewards | Official campaign documentation, reward rules, task verification guidelines |
| How concentrated is token supply after deployment? | Concentration affects governance and economic resilience | Token distribution data, vesting schedules, wallet concentration metrics |
| Does the fee model support long-term network sustainability? | Fee flows affect validators, treasury, burns, stakers, and infrastructure roles | Fee distribution documentation, on-chain fee data, treasury records |
| How accessible is governance participation? | Governance affects protocol updates and economic decision-making | Proposal rules, voting records, quorum data, participation rates |
| What metrics should be monitored after mainnet maturity? | Empirical data is needed before strong conclusions can be made | On-chain analytics, validator data, transaction data, governance history |

## Hypotheses for Future Evaluation

| Hypothesis | Current Treatment | Future Validation Method |
|---|---|---|
| QOR utility may support network participation if tied to real transaction, staking, governance, and service usage | Research hypothesis | Measure transaction fees, staking activity, governance participation, and service usage |
| Validator and delegation incentives may improve network reliability if rewards are sustainable and participation is distributed | Research hypothesis | Track validator count, uptime, stake distribution, and reward concentration |
| Light node incentives may broaden infrastructure participation if requirements and verification are clear | Research hypothesis | Track light node participation, uptime, commission records, and operator retention |
| Quest-based incentives may help early onboarding but may not predict long-term ecosystem maturity | Research hypothesis | Compare task activity with later contributor retention and repository activity |
| Tokenomics sustainability depends on balancing fee demand, reward emissions, treasury use, governance, and user activity | Open research question | Longitudinal analysis after mainnet data becomes available |

## Evidence Categories

| Evidence Category | Use in Chapter 5 | Reliability Level |
|---|---|---|
| Official QoreChain documentation | Defines stated tokenomics, fee flows, staking, governance, and infrastructure roles | Primary project source |
| Public repository materials | Supports technical and documentation-level claims | Primary project/source evidence |
| On-chain data | Confirms actual usage, transactions, fees, balances, validator participation, and governance behavior | Empirical evidence |
| Governance records | Supports analysis of decision-making participation and concentration | Empirical governance evidence |
| Community task data | Shows early engagement and onboarding activity | Community evidence, not protocol-level proof |
| External audits or third-party reports | Supports independent validation of economic or security assumptions | Independent evidence |

## Risk Categories

| Risk Area | Description | Suggested Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Reward uncertainty | Early participation tasks may be confused with guaranteed token rewards | Clearly separate points, commissions, and confirmed token rewards |
| Supply concentration | Large holders may influence governance and market behavior | Require post-deployment distribution data |
| Unsustainable emissions | Rewards may exceed long-term network demand if not balanced by real usage | Require emission schedules and fee data |
| Weak fee demand | Low transaction activity may weaken economic sustainability | Monitor transaction count, fee volume, and application activity |
| Governance concentration | Voting power may be dominated by a small group | Track voting participation and stake distribution |
| Infrastructure centralization | Validators, RPCs, or light node participation may become concentrated | Track validator set, node geography, and operator diversity |
| Short-term task behavior | Campaign incentives may produce temporary activity without durable contribution | Compare task completion with long-term contributor retention |

| Delegator count | Measures accessibility of staking participation |

| Governance proposal count | Measures governance activity |

## Academic Position

Chapter 5 should remain evidence-aware. Tokenomics can be discussed as a system of incentives, but strong claims require confirmed documentation and public network data. Until those sources are available, QoreChain tokenomics should be treated as a staged research topic that requires future empirical validation after mainnet maturity.
